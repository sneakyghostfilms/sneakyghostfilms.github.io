                    DEATH OF A SILVERY ANGEL

                            Scott Danzig




















                            FADE IN:

EXT. BACKYARD PATIO -- NIGHT

A white tablecloth flows onto stone pavers, with a string of
lights beneath the sycamore's canopy. Wine, cheeses, fruits,
veggies. A telescope points skyward.

NORA (40s, elegant) sets two glasses. Checks the sky against
her watch. Satisfied.


INT. HOUSE -- AT THE FRENCH DOORS -- CONTINUOUS

Warm light reflects off the glass doors. NEIL (30s) in a knit
cap embroidered with crescent moons. A small gift bag in one
hand. He hums.

He catches his reflection in the pane. Adjusts the hat. Smiles
at himself. Tries the smile again, smaller. Exhales.

He pushes the door open.


EXT. BACKYARD PATIO -- CONTINUOUS

                    NEIL
          Nora. Wow. Look at this.
              (admires ambiance)
          Brought you something.

He hands her a small ceramic moon. She smiles thinly.

                    NORA
          Thoughtful. Sit.

They do. She pours. He fidgets with a silver crescent pendant
at his throat.

                    NEIL
          Did you know this will be the
          biggest supermoon we'll see all
          century?

                    NORA
          I think maybe longer. You don't
          date the president of the Moon
          Society without studying up.

                    NEIL
          You really know the way to my
          heart! So... you wanna join maybe?

                    NORA
          Buy me a drink first?

She looks at the wine. Neil takes the bottle. Pours.

                    NORA (CONT'D)
          Tell me, why this moon obsession?

Neil picks up a carrot from the veggie tray and tries balancing
it on his finger.

                    NEIL
          I can't really explain it. I'm a
          bit of a lone wolf, honestly, and
          the moon was always there for me in
          my darker moments.
              (beat)
          I guess night time is dark though.

Neil laughs awkwardly at his own joke and puts the carrot back.
Nora giggles.

                    NORA
          Well. People tell me I'm eccentric.

                    NEIL
          How so?

                    NORA
          I run simulations.

                    NEIL
          On a computer?

                    NORA
          Yeah. Chain reactions that start
          with something small. Then I make
          them happen.

                    NORA (CONT'D)
          It all started when my ex wrote a
          really lame poem.


INSERT -- WHITE WALL.

A MAN in an earnest pose, eyes closed, hand on heart.

                    MAN
          ...my silvery angel of the night --

                    NEIL
          What was the poem about?

                    NORA
          I started experimenting with
          simulations. Social situations.
          Then markets -- stocks, crypto. I
          spent it all on computing power.

                    NEIL
              (leaning in)
          How far did you get?

                    NORA (V.O.)
          Eventually I threw a pebble at my
          ex.


INSERT -- MAN LOOKING UP.

The MAN again, gazing skyward with longing.

                    MAN
          I yearn for your caress, my shining
          pearl goddess --

A pebble flies in from off-screen. THUNK. He flinches.

                    NORA (V.O.)
          Five years later a man had a bad
          day with a toilet.


INSERT -- HOME BATHROOM.

A man's legs in front of an overflowing toilet, water pooling
onto his shoes and attache.

                    MAN (O.S.)
          Damn it! Yeah, I'm gonna be in
          late.


BACK TO PATIO.

Neil laughs, listening intently.

                    NORA (V.O.)
          Ten years later, a satellite
          drifted.


INSERT -- ORBIT.

A satellite slowly rotating against the black of space.


BACK TO PATIO.

Neil's amusement fades as Nora rises and walks to the telescope.
She bends to the eyepiece.

                    NORA
          An asteroid nobody was watching
          got nudged. And took its time.

She straightens, looks at Neil.

                    NORA (CONT'D)
          Can you guess what the poem was
          about?

She tilts her head toward the eyepiece, inviting. Neil crosses
and leans over it.


NEIL'S POV -- THROUGH THE TELESCOPE.

An asteroid flies into frame, shrinking as it plunges toward
the moon.


BACK TO PATIO.

Neil steps back from the eyepiece. Looks up at the sky.

                    NEIL
              (quiet)
          I need the moon.

                    NORA
              (glaring at the moon)
          I don't know what that rock has
          that I don't!

                    NEIL
          Wait, why isn't your ex here?

                    NORA
          He just couldn't go on.
              (mock-swoon)
          Wanted to be up there with his
          precious silvery angel.

She gestures upward.

                    NEIL
          But... why am I --

Nora whirls.

                    NORA
          You... like that thing! I'm gonna
          watch YOUR face when it happens!

Neil shakes his head in resignation. Nora grins toward the moon,
expectant.

Silence, a bit too long.

She glances over.

                    NORA
          Wait, wha --

                                                   CUT TO:


INSERT -- FANGS.

Human lips pulling back over inhuman teeth. A snarl.

                                                   CUT TO:


EXT. SKY -- NIGHT

The full moon. Huge. Silent.

A wet crunch. A scream, cut short. Snarling. Chewing. A HOWL --
long, triumphant, grieving.

A soundless FLASH beyond the world. The moon THUNKS out of
existence. Black.

The howl breaks, mid-note, into a human COUGH.

                                                   CUT TO:


EXT. BACKYARD PATIO -- CONTINUOUS

NEIL kneels. Shirtless. Blood on his mouth, his chest, his
hands. The moon hat lies near him, crescents facing up.

He looks up at the empty sky, as a single tear falls.

He snivels and wipes his nose. He keeps staring.


                            FADE OUT.



                            FADE IN:

INT. THERAPY OFFICE -- DAY

NEIL sits on a chaise lounge. The knit cap with crescent
moons rests in his lap. His thumb works one of the
embroidered points.

                    NEIL
          Once a month. My whole life.
          I was somebody. Something.
          Magnificent. And now every
          idiot on Earth lost the same
          moon. They get to grieve.
          They post about the TIDES.
          They lost a rock. I lost her.


                            FADE OUT.
